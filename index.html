About The System

This project introduces an innovative embedded system for controlling streetlights using an ESP32 microcontroller, designed to enhance energy efficiency while maintaining optimal lighting conditions. The system intelligently manages LED lighting based on ambient light levels, time of day, and motion detection, ensuring lights operate only when necessary.

Built with sustainability in focus, the smart streetlight controller prioritizes real time ambient light conditions over fixed time schedules, preventing unnecessary activation during daylight hours. It functions within a flexible night window (6 PM to 7 AM) and includes safeguards for rare extreme low-light conditions during the day such as during storms. Even during night time, if the surroundings are sufficiently bright, the system keeps the lights OFF.

System background

The current street lighting systems operate on fixed schedules, turning lights ON or OFF at predetermined times, without considering actual ambient light levels. This approach leads to energy inefficiency and reduced reliability, as lights may remain on when there is sufficient natural daylight or turn OFF while it is still dark. In some cases, lights even stay off during daytime hours despite the dark conditions such as during a storm. Additionally, streetlights operate at full brightness throughout the night, regardless of activity levels . During stormy or overcast daytime conditions, the lights remain OFF despite significantly reduced visibility.

Lights ON

If the lights are ON it may be due to nighttime darkness or unusually low daylight levels, such as during a storm. In such cases the system activates lights at a dimmed brightness level when no motion is detected, conserving energy. When motion is detected, the lights automatically switch to full brightness . This approach is specifically useful during late-night hours -such as around midnight -when road usage is minimal. By dimming lights during inactivity and increasing brightness only when necessary, the system ensures both energy efficiency and safety for road users.

System Logic Summary

The controller uses an ESP32 to process inputs from an LDR (ambient light), PIR (motion),and DS3231 RTC(time),controlling a 10mm LED with PWM dimming. The LED activates only when dark (LDR > 2500) during 6 PM–7 AM or in extreme darkness (LDR > 3500) during daytime, with brightness at 50% (no motion) or 100% (motion). The streetlight controller handles all scenarios at 6 PM and 7 AM effectively, prioritizing LDR to avoid activation in daylight (e.g., 6 PM, 6:59 AM) and ensuring lighting when dark (e.g., 6:57 PM, 6:59 AM if dark). The 5 PM–7 AM window, storm exception, and motion dimming make it energy-efficient and practical, ideal for scaling to real streetlights

Main System Components

The system is made up of an ESP32 microcontroller that works as the computer of the system. It has a Passive Infra Red (PIR) sensor that detects motion such if the Lights are ON, it switches to full brightness and dims when no motion is detected. It has a Light Dependent Resistor whose resistance increases as the darkness levels increase, so its used to detect darkness. It has a Real Time Clock (RTC) module used to keep track of the current time and date. It has two LED ligts to act as streetlights that turn on when all conditions are meant. The system uses a potentiometer to vary the resistance through the LDR.

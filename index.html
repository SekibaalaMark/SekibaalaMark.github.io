About The System

This project presents an innovative embedded system for controlling streetlights using an ESP32 microcontroller, designed to optimize energy efficiency while maintaining optimal lighting conditions. The system intelligently controls LED lighting based on ambient light conditions, time of day, and motion detection, ensuring lights operate only when needed.

Built with sustainability in mind, our smart streetlight controller prioritizes ambient light conditions over fixed time schedules, avoiding unnecessary activation during daylight hours. The system operates within a flexible night window (6 PM to 7 AM) and includes safeguards for rare daytime extreme darkness conditions such as storms. Even if its night time, yet it still bright enough our systems keeps the lights OFF.

The current street lighting system operates on a fixed schedule, turning lights on or off at predetermined times without accounting for actual ambient light levels. This approach leads to energy inefficiency and reduced reliability, as lights may remain on when there is sufficient natural daylight or turn off while it is still dark or even stay off during day hours yet itâ€™s a dark day for example in a storm. Additionally, the lights operate at full brightness throughout the night, even during periods when there is no activity going on. During stormy or overcast daytime conditions, the lights remain off despite reduced visibility.

The controller uses an ESP32 to process inputs from an LDR (ambient light), PIR (motion),and DS3231 RTC(time),controlling a 10mm LED with PWM dimming. The LED activates only when dark (LDR > 2000) during 6 PMâ€“7 AM or in extreme darkness (LDR > 3500) during daytime, with brightness at 50% (no motion) or 100% (motion). The streetlight controller handles all scenarios at 6 PM and 7 AM effectively, prioritizing LDR to avoid activation in daylight (e.g., 6 PM, 6:59 AM) and ensuring lighting when dark (e.g., 6:57 PM, 6:59 AM if dark). The 5 PMâ€“7 AM window, storm exception, and motion dimming make it energy-efficient and practical, ideal for scaling to real streetlights

Main System Components

The system is made up of an ESP32 microcontroller that works as the computer of the system. It has a Passive Infra Red (PIR) sensor that detects motion such if the Lights are on it switches to full brightness and dims when no motion is detected. It has a Light Dependent Resistor whose resistance increases as the darkness levels increase, so its used to detect darkness or brigthness. It has a Real Time Clock (RTC) module used to keep track of the current time and date. It has two LED ligts to act as streetlights that turn on when all conditions are meant. The system uses a potentiometer to vary the resistance through the LDR
